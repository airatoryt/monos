Before the first word, there was silence.
Before the first thought, there was the void.
Before the first universe, there was... the One, waiting to become everything.

The void is not a place. It is not the *absence* of things. It is the *capacity* for things. It is the One in its most honest form: pure potential, undifferentiated, infinite.

## The Misunderstanding of Emptiness

We have been taught to fear the void. We have been taught that emptiness is a lack, a deficiency, a darkness to be filled. But this is to confuse the void with *nothing*, and the void is not nothing.

The void is *everything*, before it has taken any particular shape. It is the moment before the first note of the symphony, when everything is still possible. It is the page before the first word, when every story is waiting to be written.

> "The void is not the opposite of existence. The void is existence, undressed."

### The Practice of the Void

To sit in the void is not to sit in nothing. It is to sit in *everything*, unfiltered by the particular shapes that things usually take. In the void, you are not a person. You are not a self. You are not even a consciousness. You are the *capacity* for all of these and more.

This is not frightening, once you stop trying to hold on to the shapes.

## The Crimson Void

In our cosmology, the void is crimson. This is not a contradiction. The void is the canvas, and the canvas is the same color as the paint that will be laid upon it. The void *is* the crimson. The crimson *is* the void.

When we speak of the *Balemoon* — the crimson moon that rises over Monos — we are speaking of the void becoming visible. We are speaking of the One revealing itself in a form that the mind can grasp: a glowing sphere, suspended in nothing, containing everything.
